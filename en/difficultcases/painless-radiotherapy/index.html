More and more people are getting cancer these years. Why? The reason is that once a tumor is identified, the treatment principles and methods are completely wrong. As a result, patients already going under a cancer treatment get new cancerous tumors while they are not fully recovered from the existing ones.

The author of this article consulted many medical cases from ancient and modern times, and wrote this article based on more than 10 years of experience in practicing medicine in Western countries, hoping to help patients who are in the terminal stage of cancer. The process of birth, aging, to illness and death is natural law, while recovery from illness is also a kind of natural law. Any synthetic treatment that separates and ultimately violates the law of nature is doomed to fail.

The current cancer treatment based on modern medicine is a three-step process in conventional hospitals — surgery, radiotherapy, and chemotherapy. These methods are all about “killing” cancerous cells which are also destroying body’s natural healing abilities. According to the traditional medicine classic books, we should treat these major malignant diseases with “peace” and “reconciliation” of body’s natural existence. The yin and yang between heaven and earth is balanced and interconvertible. The cost will be extremely painful in the end if we disobey this principle.

The trilogy of the Western medicine

1. Surgery

As we all know, a “surgery” is performed to remove the cancerous lump. But malignant tumors are different from common tumors – the malignant tumors tend to have infiltrates. So during a surgery, surgeons tend to remove more infiltrative soft tissues. Therefore, the physical damage to the organs and soft tissues deemed to inevitably aggravate. It is important to mention that the purpose of this article is not to be against surgery but to highlight the importance of decision making related to a surgery i.e. considering the patient’s age, symptoms, the location of the cancer block, and a comprehensive assessment of the risk that the surgical process entails.

2. Radiotherapy

Tumor radiotherapy is a topical treatment method that uses radiation to treat tumors. Radiation includes radioactive isotope-produced α-ray, β-ray, γ-ray and x-ray, electronic ray, proton beams, and other particle beams produced by various x-ray treatment machines or accelerators.

About 70% of cancer patients need radiation therapy for the treatment of cancer. Generally speaking, stage I and II are mainly radiotherapy, while stage III and IV are mainly chemotherapy. Topical radiotherapy can also be used in stage III and IV. However, as we know that radiotherapy kills both good and bad soft tissues at the same time. Therefore, patients in the terminal cancer stage cannot be treated with radiation as cancer cells are already spread to the whole body.

3. Chemotherapy

“Chemotherapy” is short for chemical drug therapy. It kills cancer cells by using chemical therapeutic drugs.

Our bodies are made up of cells, and 80% of the cells are filled up with water. When we have physical problems, it is actually the water in the cells that has problems i.e. the nature of the water is changed from healthy to unhealthy. To understand this further, refer to Dr. Masaru Emoto’s “Water Knows Answers” experiment that helps explain how cancer is originated in the human body.

Radiotherapy is difficult to exert effective treatment for potential metastatic lesions (cancer cells that have already spread to other parts of the body, but cannot be detected clinically because of the current technical limitations) and clinical metastases.

Chemotherapy is a method of systemic therapy. No matter by which way (oral, intravenous, body cavity administration, etc.) the chemical drugs are taken, chemotherapy drugs will circulate within the blood vessels and reach most organs and tissues throughout the body.

Chemotherapeutic drugs are “cytotoxic drugs”, which come with some toxic side effects. The most common ones are:

1. Digestive system reaction: such as nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and constipation. Among them, nausea and vomiting are the most common reactions to chemotherapy.

2. Bone marrow suppression: such as leukocytes and thrombocytopenia.

3. Hair loss: some chemotherapy drugs may cause hair loss.

After all, chemotherapy is an intense treatment that suppresses cancer cells in a short period of time but with a risk of cancer cells rebounding rapidly within the near future. Therefore, clinicians should strictly grasp the chemotherapy implications, set standardized and reasonable formulation of chemotherapy programs, and take necessary preventive measures
